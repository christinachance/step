Background

I grew up in Hartford, Connecticut with my mother and sister. Both of my parents are immigrants from Jamaica so culturally that's how I was raised. As a child, I enjoyed dancing, playing piano, martial arts and learning new things. I attened Jumoke Academy for elementay and Jumoke Academy Honors SMART for middle school. In middle school, I joined an after school and summer program called Hartford Youth Scholars' Stepping Stone Academy. I met some of my best friends through that program, and it helped me get into my highschool, the Peddie School in Hightstown, New Jersey.

At Peddie, I was a prefect, a tour guide, leader of several organizations, and a thrower for the Track and Field team . During highschool, I became interested in the arts and as my final senior arts exhibit, I exhibited my friend group as if we were a family, with me being the grandmother. I created a living room and shot several photos of my friends in familial scenes. It was easily my favorite work. Since then, I have continued to paint and photograpgh in my free time.

I currently attened Emory univeristy in Atlanta, Georgia. At Emory, I am the Treasurer of the Association of Caribbean Students and Educators (ACES), Sophmore Advisor for the 2019-2020 year, a Residential Advisor for the 2020-2021 year, and an IMSD (Iniative for Maximizing Student Devlopment) Scholar. I am also the founder and President of BLIS (Black and Latinx in STEM). This org is like my baby and holds alot of significance to me. The rentention rate for Black and Latinx students in STEM is low, and I have witnessed several friends change their career or major paths because they weren't successful in their STEM classes or felt like they lacked support or guidance from these departments. The goal of this organization is to create a community amongst these students, connect them with faculty and professionals in their desired fields, and help ensure their success in their fields through career development workshop amd mentorship.

In term of my career path, I am apart of Girls for Technology, a Hartford-based group that introduces young women to Computer Science and Tech. Through this program, I myself was introduced to computer science and also to Google. Since then I attened Google's Computer Science Summer Institute in 2018 and was Engineering Practicum intern the Summer of 2019 in New York.

Why I do Art

Growing up, I was never truly creative. I took alot of art courses and after school classes but I wnever had the creative edge. However, I was always someone who enjoyed going through the process of something and seeing the final results. I was always detail-oriented and that showed in many of my interests. As I got older, I saw all my interets overlap. With art, I viewed it as a process and less like a creative form. Art still has a calming and stress-relieving affect on me,and still allowed me yto work on my focus. When I discovered this is highschool, I held onto it as long as I could. While I can't continue art classes in college, I have found that computer science has taken its place. I still do art when I'm bored or whenever my body tells me its time to paint something, but it is no longer a necassary part of my life.
